Number 1

Description

This dish is known as "Number 1" because at one point in time I was eating it at least three days a week and it was the most frequent thing I cooked. It developed slowly over many iterations into the dish you see before you. It's vaguely reminiscent of a Thai peanut sauce noodle dish but much more Americanized and bastardized. The peanut butter lends it a creamy texture while the soy sauce gives it a lot of umami.

Ingredients

  • Pasta (preferably farfalle)
  • Broccoli
  • Red pepper
  • Mushrooms
  • Field Roast sausage (apple sage flavor)
  • Garlic
  • Yellow onion
  • Peanut butter
  • Soy sauce
  • Rice vinegar
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Slice the Field Roast thinly, on a bias
  2. Mince the garlic and chop the onion
  3. Cut the broccoli crowns off the stems and seperate the crowns into bite-sized pieces
  4. Peel the stems and cut them into bite-sized pieces
  5. Chop the red pepper and slice the mushrooms
  6. Boil water in a large pot, adding a heaping chunk of salt
  7. Warm a small pan on medium heat
  8. When the pan is hot, add some oil and let it warm up
  9. When the oil is hot, add the sliced Field Roast, stirring occasionally
  10. In a separate pot, heat oil and add garlic
  11. As the garlic warms, add the onion
  12. Once the onion starts to become transparent, add the broccoli stems
  13. After the broccoli stems have cooked a little, add the crowns
  14. When the broccoli has softened a little, add the peppers and mushrooms
  15. Add the cooked Field Roast to the pot of vegetables and keep warm on low heat
  16. When the pot is boiling, add the pasta
  17. After several minutes, taste the pasta. When it is still a little crunchy (just before al dente), drain while reserving a small amount of the pasta water
  18. Combine the pasta, reserved pasta water, and the pot of vegetables and Field Roast
  19. Add a heaping gob of peanut butter (seriously, way more than you probably think), a fair amount of soy sauce, and a healthy dash of rice vinegar
  20. Continue to stir and simmer on medium heat until enough water has boiled off that the sauce appears thick
  21. Let cool and enjoy your first number 1!
